How to Create IME

This question is out of step with "IMM Programming", but somehow I am often asked...
Are there so many needs to create an IME? Unbelievable.

Especially for Europian/American readers, please remember that Windows' IME is NOT a way to input a foreign lanuguage. It is orginally a way to input native DBCS characters on the system, that is, double-byte Japanese characters on Japanese system, Chinese characters on Chinese system and so on. Input method editors are available only on Asian version of Windows.

Usually creating IME is a big work. Japanese IME vendors including MSKK always make effort to fix their bugs, develop their conversion efficiency, satisfy their users needs and feelings. And no software vendors, except those which have been well-known as their famous IMEs, are going to participate in this area. If you are planning to make a Japanese IME easily, I advice you to stop. I can tell nothing for other languages (how can I because I can't even read them?).

I won't explain how to develop an IME step by step. That's impossible and a burden, and my experience comes to be somewhat old. Instead, I will show you a few official information on IME development. Please look around and investigate by yourself.

  • First, you should get Far East Device Driver Kit (FE DDK).
  • Read and understand "Windows 95 Multilingual IME" section in DDK.
    In older version of DDK, it was separated into 2 MS-Word documents, imeime_1.doc and imeime_2.doc.
  • Look into the sample IME "FAKEIME" in DDK to implement the IME interface.
  • See other IME's behavior, especially one provided by Microsoft, especially in testing and debugging processes.

That's all. Actually, I had developed UI parts of a Japanese IME using only these information. Sorry that I can't help you any more unless it becomes my "official" work again (and will never, I think).
